:::warning[Too Long, Didn't Read]

We're forking Radash, a modern TypeScript utility library with 100K+ weekly downloads and 4K stars on GitHub, which is sadly unmaintained. We want your support to help Radashi reach its full potential of a decentralized, community-driven utility library. Read on to learn more about Radashi's vision.

:::

Radashi wants to be your new favorite utility library for TypeScript. Instead of taking the safe route of rewriting Lodash into a modern library like others have done, Radashi builds on ~3 years of development by its predecessor (Radash), which dares to rethink what a utility library for TypeScript should be.

As we all know, Lodash is a great library, but its age is showing. Not just in terms of the codebase, but also the mindset. We love Lodash for its pragmatic approach to (more or less) idiomatic JavaScript. Unfortunately, Lodash's complexity has cost it a sustainable future that allows it to keep up with the pace of modern JavaScript. It's more than that, though. Lodash is built with a mindset that serves an ever-shrinking audience of developers; developers who wanted a toolkit that “just works” and didn't care if it was bloated or hard to modify.

Radashi has a big vision, one bigger than its predecessor's. It's not just a library. It's a community of like-minded TypeScript developers that **doesn't revolve around a single person**. Radashi is a new mindset, one with design principles that are explicitly defined in a living document, [Our Ethos](/our-ethos).

At Radashi, we believe that **there is no “one size fits all”** when it comes to utility libraries. But that doesn't mean we, as a community, should be fragmented in our efforts. Using the Radashi ethos as our common ground, a new layer of collaboration is born. Radashi aims to be a **solid foundation** for you to customize and extend to your heart's content. We've discovered that if we all organize our utility libraries in the same way, we can optimize shareability and collaboration.

Radashi is a utility library with no gate-keeper. There are stewards, like myself, who help keep the library aligned with the Radashi ethos, but the community ultimately decides the direction of the library. One way of doing that is by proposing changes to the ethos itself. The other way is by using Radashi's pull requests tab on GitHub as a town square for lightly-moderated sharing of functions that adhere to Radashi's ethos (more on this in a later post). I'm developing tools for easily adding PRs to your own Radashi and also for easily extending Radashi without the hassle of staying “in sync” with the official `main` branch. These tools include a CLI and a VSCode extension, which are both almost finished.

If this sounds like promising vision, we need your support. Even if it's just a star on GitHub or a like/share on social media, it helps us get the word out there. Also, your contributions are extremely valuable to us. We are nearing the release of v13.0.0, and now is the perfect time to get involved.
